## Your Time Is Worth More Than You Think

Here's a number that might surprise you: the average American spends roughly **six hours per week** on household cleaning tasks. That's according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, and for families with kids or pets, it's often much higher.

Six hours a week adds up to **312 hours a year**. That's nearly eight full 40-hour work weeks. Imagine what you could do with eight extra weeks every year — pick up a hobby, launch a side project, spend more time with your kids, or simply rest.

Now think about what your time is worth. Even at a modest hourly rate, say $20 per hour, those 312 hours represent **$6,240 in lost value** every single year. For professionals in Logan UT earning more than that, the number climbs fast.

We're not saying your time at home isn't valuable. We're saying you deserve the choice in how you spend it.

## The Hidden Dollar Cost of DIY Cleaning

Even when you do the cleaning yourself, it's not free in the financial sense either. Here's what most Cache Valley households spend on cleaning supplies and equipment over the course of a year:

- **All-purpose cleaners, disinfectants, and specialty products:** $150–$300/year
- **Paper towels, sponges, microfiber cloths, and scrub brushes:** $75–$150/year
- **Vacuum cleaner maintenance or replacement:** $100–$400/year
- **Mop heads, broom replacements, and bucket supplies:** $30–$75/year
- **Specialty items** (grout cleaner, stainless steel polish, glass cleaner): $50–$100/year

That's anywhere from **$400 to over $1,000 per year** just on supplies and equipment — and that's before we count the gas to run to Smith's or Walmart for the stuff you ran out of mid-clean.
