## Insurance, Training, and Professionalism

This one might feel less exciting to talk about, but it's critically important — especially for commercial spaces. You want to make sure your cleaning company carries proper liability insurance. If something gets damaged or someone is injured on your property, you need to know you're protected.

Beyond insurance, ask about training. Are their cleaners trained on different surface types, chemical safety, and equipment handling? A truly professional team won't just show up with a mop and a spray bottle. They'll understand how to care for your specific flooring, furniture, and fixtures.
